# Product Brief: Lolos

## Executive Summary

**Lolos** (Indonesian: "pass through") is an AI-powered SaaS platform that helps Indonesian job seekers build ATS-optimized CVs through natural conversation — not forms. In 5 minutes, a user describes their background to an AI career coach in Bahasa Indonesia, and the platform produces a recruiter-grade CV optimized for the specific ATS platforms Indonesian companies actually use.

The global resume builder market is $8.86 billion (2025), growing at 7.4% CAGR, with the AI sub-segment growing nearly twice as fast at 13.1%. Indonesia has 4.5 million university graduates per year, 74.9 million Gen Z entering the workforce, and 16.26% youth unemployment — yet **zero dedicated AI resume tools serve this market in Bahasa Indonesia.** Every existing competitor (Rezi, Kickresume, Canva, Teal) is English-only, priced for Western markets ($7-35/month), and optimized for Western ATS systems (Greenhouse, Workday) — not the Indonesian platforms (Talenta, Mekari, LinovHR) that actually screen Indonesian CVs.

**The wedge:** Conversational AI onboarding in natural Bahasa Indonesia. No competitor has this. The user experience mirrors ChatGPT — users talk about their career, the AI extracts and structures their data, and a complete ATS-optimized CV emerges. This is not a translated product; it is built Indonesian-first, from the "Kak" AI persona to GoPay/QRIS payment to WhatsApp-native sharing.

## The Problem

**CVs are a "translation tax" between human careers and machine screening.** Indonesian job seekers face a compounding disadvantage:

1. **75% of CVs are rejected by ATS before human review** — globally. In Indonesia, this number is likely higher because ATS adoption is accelerating (Talenta alone grew from 250 to 1,000+ enterprise clients in 18 months), but job seeker awareness is near zero. Most fresh graduates do not know ATS exists.

2. **Formatting traps silently destroy applications.** Two-column layouts, tables, centered text, headers/footers, and text boxes all scramble ATS parsing. Indonesian CV conventions (photo, KTP, personal details) often conflict with ATS-safe formatting (single-column, no graphics, standard section headers). Users do not know the rules, and no tool teaches them in their language.

3. **Manual customization is exhausting.** Active job seekers apply to 10-50+ positions per cycle. Tailoring each CV to each job description — adjusting keywords, reordering sections, rewriting bullet points — is hours of repetitive work. Most give up and spray the same CV everywhere.

4. **The feedback loop is broken.** Applicants receive responses on only 5-15% of applications. Rejections come with zero explanation. Users do not know whether they were rejected for qualifications, formatting, keywords, or something else. This creates learned helplessness — "I've applied to 50 jobs and heard nothing. Maybe I'm not good enough."

5. **Every existing tool is built for someone else.** Western pricing ($7-35/month) is 2-10x what Indonesian job seekers can afford. Western languages exclude 270 million Indonesian speakers. Western payment methods (credit cards) have 2% penetration in Indonesia. Western ATS knowledge does not translate to Talenta, Mekari, or LinovHR.

## The Solution

**Lolos** replaces the resume-building form with a conversation. The user talks. The AI listens, asks follow-up questions, extracts structured data, and builds an ATS-optimized CV.

**Core experience:**

1. **AI Interview.** A 5-15 minute conversational onboarding where the AI career coach ("Kak") asks about experience, education, skills, achievements, and career goals — in natural Bahasa Indonesia. The AI adapts questions based on career stage (fresh graduate vs. executive vs. freelancer), detects when answers are thin and probes deeper, and allows skipping. The experience feels like talking to a helpful career coach, not filling a form.

2. **ATS-Optimized Output.** The platform generates a CV that is validated against real Indonesian ATS platforms (Talenta, Mekari, LinovHR) — single-column format, standard fonts, proper section headers, no tables or graphics that break parsing. The user sees their ATS score (0-100 across six dimensions: keyword match, formatting, completeness, readability, metrics impact, optimization) and gets specific, actionable improvement suggestions.

3. **Multi-Template, Multi-Version.** Users choose from ATS-safe templates optimized for different contexts (corporate, tech startup, creative, academic). They maintain multiple CV versions for different roles. Switching templates does not lose data.

4. **Job Match Analysis.** Users paste a job description; the AI compares their CV, shows a match percentage, identifies missing keywords and skills, and suggests specific improvements — turning a generic CV into a targeted application in minutes.

5. **Export Anywhere.** PDF for global applications. DOCX for Indonesian HR departments (97% parsing accuracy vs. 76% for PDF on Indonesian ATS). Shareable link with privacy controls (public, password-protected, expiry, view tracking).

## Vision

If Lolos succeeds, it becomes the default career companion for every Indonesian professional — not just a resume builder.

**Year 2-3:** The platform expands across the career lifecycle: AI interview practice, salary insights and negotiation coaching, LinkedIn optimization, portfolio building, and a recruiter dashboard for employer-side matching. The resume becomes a living career profile — updated continuously, optimized for every application, portable across platforms. The data moat deepens: millions of Indonesian resumes provide training data for models that understand the Indonesian job market better than any global competitor.

**Year 5:** Lolos is the gateway to professional opportunity in Indonesia. Employers use the platform to discover candidates. Universities integrate it into career readiness programs. The AI understands what makes Indonesian professionals succeed — not just what makes a CV parse correctly — and guides users toward career outcomes, not just better documents.
