## Research Overview

Comprehensive market research covering four pillars:
1. ATS resume builder market in Indonesia
2. AI SaaS adoption and willingness to pay in Indonesia
3. Indonesian job seeker behavior and hiring trends
4. Competitor deep-dive: Rezi, Teal, Canva, Kickresume, and local players

**Key Findings:** The global resume builder market is **$8.86B** (2025), growing at 7.4% CAGR, with AI sub-segment at **$1.47B** growing 13.1% CAGR. Indonesia represents a **wide-open market** — zero dedicated AI resume builders exist with Bahasa Indonesia support, GoPay/QRIS payment, or Indonesian ATS platform validation. Gen Z (74.9M, 27.94% of population) dominates the workforce pipeline but faces 16.26% youth unemployment. 82% of Indonesians use AI but only 32% trust AI transactions. The Indonesian HR/ATS market is growing rapidly (Talenta: 1K enterprise clients, 4x in 18 months), meaning more CVs are automated-screened — and no tool helps candidates optimize for these systems. **No competitor** provides conversational AI onboarding in Bahasa Indonesia, confirming a first-mover opportunity with defensible moats.

### Psychographic Profiles

**Values and Beliefs:**
- Gen Z places strong emphasis on **value congruence** (Person-Organization Fit) — they apply to companies whose values match their own
- Career growth and learning opportunities often outweigh pure salary considerations
- **Transparency** in recruitment process is highly valued — opaque processes create distrust
- Growing awareness of **ATS systems** among job seekers, but still low understanding of how they work
- **#KaburAjaDulu** sentiment reveals a belief that opportunities abroad are better — tools that improve local competitiveness address a real emotional need

**Lifestyle Preferences:**
- Mobile-first: 90%+ of internet usage via smartphone
- Social media integrated into all life aspects, including job search
- Heavy WhatsApp usage (96% penetration among smartphone users)
- Short-form video consumption (TikTok, Instagram Reels) influences career aspirations

**Attitudes Toward AI Tools:**
- **82% of Indonesian consumers have used AI** for digital activities (YouGov & Visa, 2025)
- **But only 32% trust AI** to complete actual transactions — data security and fee transparency are top concerns
- **79% of Indonesian SMEs already use AI** in business — high baseline AI literacy
- Users are experiencing "AI fatigue" — craving **authenticity and human connection** in AI products

**Attitudes Toward Career Tools:**
- Willing to try free tools extensively before paying
- Strong preference for tools that "feel local" — Bahasa Indonesia, local payment, local testimonials
- Trust signals: university endorsements, testimonials from peers, visible user counts
- "Bantuan karir" (career help) perceived as more valuable than "bikin CV" (make CV) — positioning matters

### Customer Interaction Patterns

**Research & Discovery:**
- Job seekers use 2-3 platforms simultaneously during active job search
- Jobstreet remains the most trusted platform with the healthiest sentiment ecosystem (Humayyah & Siregar, 2025)
- Glints has growing mindshare among tech/startup talent but with sentiment polarization
- LinkedIn growing among professionals but still smaller than local platforms
- WhatsApp groups and Telegram channels are major (untracked) job discovery channels
- TikTok and Instagram increasingly used for career content discovery

**Application Decision Process:**
1. Discover job via platform or social media
2. Research company (employer brand, values, reputation)
3. Assess Person-Organization Fit
4. Prepare application materials (CV, cover letter)
5. Apply — often across 10-50+ positions per cycle
6. Track responses (poorly — most users have no tracking system)

**Post-Application Behavior:**
- Job seekers receive responses on only 5-15% of applications
- Rejection typically comes with no feedback — users don't know WHY they were rejected
- Most users have no understanding of ATS screening as the rejection cause
- Users who understand ATS are more likely to invest in resume optimization

**Loyalty Factors:**
- Tool effectiveness (did I get more interviews?) is the #1 retention driver
- Time saved vs manual CV creation
- Multi-CV management for different job applications
- Community/social proof — seeing peers succeed

**Churn Triggers:**
- Landing a job → stop using the tool
- Perceived lack of results after multiple applications
- Pricing friction (credit exhaustion before value realization)
- Better free alternatives

## Customer Pain Points and Needs

### Customer Challenges and Frustrations

**Primary Frustrations (Global + Indonesia):**

| Pain Point | Severity | Detail |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| **ATS "Black Hole"** | Critical | **~75% of applications rejected by ATS** before human review. Job seekers apply to 40-156 jobs with zero callbacks. No rejection feedback. |
| **Formatting Traps** | Critical | Two-column layouts, tables, text boxes, centered text, headers/footers all cause ATS parsing failures — candidate data disappears silently |
| **Keyword Game Exhaustion** | High | "I matched every keyword. Applied to 40 roles. Heard back from 2." — real job seeker. ATS semantic matching works unreliably |
| **AI-Generated "Slop"** | High | Generic AI content ("results-driven leader", "cross-functional team") fails to differentiate. "Robots talking to robots" syndrome |
| **LinkedIn Cross-Referencing** | Growing | Modern ATS cross-references LinkedIn profiles — minor title mismatches flagged, career gaps auto-rejected |
| **Non-Linear Career Bias** | High | Career gaps, freelance periods, career pivots all disproportionately filtered. 70% of non-linear professionals perform on par with linear peers |

**Indonesia-Specific Frustrations:**

| Pain Point | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **CV Format Kebingungan** | Indonesian job seekers don't know whether to include photo, KTP, agama, status pernikahan — no standardization across companies |
| **Bahasa Inggris vs Indonesia** | CV harus bilingual untuk MNC, tapi formatting berbeda. User tidak tahu kapan pakai yang mana |
| **Fresh Graduate Paradox** | "Butuh pengalaman untuk dapat kerja, butuh kerja untuk dapat pengalaman" — tidak tahu cara mengisi CV dengan organisasi/magang |
| **No ATS Awareness** | Mayoritas fresh graduate Indonesia TIDAK tahu apa itu ATS. Mereka pikir CV tidak dipanggil karena kualifikasi, bukan karena format |
| **CV "Bonus Pack" Scams** | Growing market of low-quality "jasa bikin CV" packages that produce unparsable, overdesigned CVs |

**Frequency:** For active job seekers, these frustrations are experienced **daily** during application cycles. The average Indonesian job seeker applies to 15-30 positions before landing an interview.

## Customer Decision Processes and Journey

### Customer Decision-Making Process

The job seeker's decision journey follows a natural lifecycle that maps to career tools:

| Stage | Tool Need | Decision Trigger | Conversion Point |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|------------------|
| **Resume Creation** | CV builder, AI writing | "I need a better CV" | Free tier builds resume |
| **Apply** | Cover letter, job tracker | "I need to customize for this job" | Download/export paywall |
| **Prepare** | Mock interviews, AI coaching | "I have an interview!" | Upsell to interview prep |
| **Interview** | AI copilot, negotiation tips | "I want to negotiate" | Premium subscription |
| **Get Offers** | Offer evaluation | "Which offer is better?" | Long-term retention |

**Key insight:** The most successful PLG SaaS tools own the entire journey — free at the top, paid at the bottom. Each stage naturally leads to the next.

### Customer Journey Mapping

**Awareness Stage:**
- **Indonesia Gen Z:** Social media is the primary awareness channel — LinkedIn, TikTok, Instagram
- **Career influencers:** Vina Muliana (4M TikTok followers) and similar career content creators are trusted discovery sources
- **Search:** "buat CV online", "template CV ATS", "CV lamaran kerja" — Google is the research tool
- **Peer referral:** WhatsApp groups = dark social channel for career tool recommendations
- **University career centers:** Underutilized but trusted awareness channel

**Consideration Stage:**
- Users compare 2-3 tools before committing
- Key evaluation criteria: "Can I build a CV for free and see the result before paying?"
- Trust signals that matter: user testimonials, university endorsements, "dipakai oleh X pencari kerja"
- **Indonesian Gen Z specifically:** Social media information has stronger effect on decisions (0.706 coefficient) than company reputation (0.467)
- Time spent in consideration: 1-3 days for active job seekers, weeks for passive seekers

**Decision Stage:**
- Trigger: User hits export/download paywall → evaluates whether sunk time investment justifies payment
- The "aha moment": Seeing ATS score for the first time, realizing previous CV was failing screening
- Payment friction is the #1 decision killer in Indonesia — GoPay/QRIS must be available
- Risk mitigation behavior: Users try free tier first, test export quality, THEN decide on paid

**Purchase Stage:**
- Preferred: One-click QRIS payment via GoPay/DANA
- Alternative: Virtual account (BCA, Mandiri, BRI) for users without e-wallets
- Credit card purchase: <3% of Indonesian transactions — support but don't optimize for it
- Key: No "hidden auto-renewal" — transparent billing builds trust

**Post-Purchase Stage:**
- Active use: 1-3 months during active job search
- Natural churn: User lands job → stops using tool (acceptable, expected)
- Re-activation: User starts new job search 1-3 years later → re-subscribes
- Advocacy: WhatsApp sharing of CV result = viral acquisition channel
- Negative churn risk: User pays, gets no interview results, blames tool

## Executive Summary

### The Market Opportunity

The global resume builder market is **$8.86 billion** (2025), growing to **$12.55 billion by 2030** (7.4% CAGR). The AI resume builder sub-segment is growing nearly twice as fast at **13.1% CAGR** — from $1.47B (2025) to $5B (2035). Indonesia, the 4th most populous country with **4.5 million university graduates per year**, has **zero dedicated AI resume builders** serving its market.

### The Customer

Indonesia's Gen Z (74.9M, 27.94% of population) dominates workforce entry. They face **16.26% youth unemployment** and a "black hole" application experience — **75% of CVs rejected by ATS before human review**. They are mobile-first (90%+ smartphone internet), social media-driven (LinkedIn, TikTok, Instagram for career discovery), and trust career influencers (70%) more than traditional celebrities. Their willingness to pay for productivity tools is rising (SEA office app revenue **+245.4% YoY**), but they require local payment (GoPay, QRIS — 78% use e-wallets) and Bahasa Indonesia-first UX.

### The Competitive Gap

Nine global competitors analyzed — Rezi (4M users, $1M+ revenue), Teal (2M members, $19M raised), Kickresume, Canva, Zety, Resume.io, and others. **None offer:** Bahasa Indonesia AI conversation, Indonesian ATS platform validation (Talenta, Mekari, LinovHR), GoPay/QRIS payment, mobile-first design, or Indonesia-specific CV format guidance. All are priced $7-$35/mo for Western markets.

### The Product Window

An AI-powered, Bahasa Indonesia-first, mobile-optimized resume builder with conversational onboarding, local ATS optimization, and GoPay/QRIS payment has a **clear first-mover window**. The moats: (1) conversational AI depth that form-based competitors can't retrofit, (2) Indonesian ATS knowledge accumulation, (3) local payment + distribution (WhatsApp) integration, (4) data network effects from Indonesian resume corpus.
